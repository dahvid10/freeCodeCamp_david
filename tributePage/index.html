Dr. John Magufuli

The African visionary who challenged the status quo

Here's a timeline of Dr. Magufuli's life:

  • 1959 - Born in Chato, Tanganyika(Tanzania)
  • 1981 - Joined University of Dar es Salaam for a Diploma in Education Science, majoring in Chemistry, Mathematics, and Education
  • 1982 - Taught Chemistry and Mathematics at Sengerema Secondary School
  • 1988 - Earned Bachelor of Science in Education majoring in Chemistry and Mathematics
  • 1989 - Worked as an industrial chemist at The Nyanza Cooperative Union Limited
  • 1994 - Earned Masters degree in Chemistry from University of Dar es Salaam
  • 1995 - Elected as Member of Parliament (MP) representing Chato district, where he served as Deputy Minister of Works
  • 2000 - retains seat and promoted to a full ministerial position
  • 2006 - Appointed to Minister of Lands and Human Settlement
  • 2008 - Served as Minister of Livestock and Fisheries
  • 2009 - Earned Doctorate degree in Chemistry from University of Dar es Salaam
  • 2010 - Served as Minister of Works
  • 2015 - Won Presidential election where he:
    • Imposed measures to curb government spending: barring unnecessary foreign travel by government officials, using cheaper vehicles, and facilities, reduced his own salary from US$15,000/month to US$4,000/month
    • Suspended country's Independence Day festivities to favour national cleanup campaign to reduce the spread of cholera
    • Amended laws governing the award of mining contracts, giving Tanzania the right to renegotiate or terminate them in the event of proven fraud
  • 2021 - John Magufuli died because of long-standing heart problems, rumors spread of him contracting Covid-19.
    • Surviving him are his wife, Janeth Magufuli and their seven children.
